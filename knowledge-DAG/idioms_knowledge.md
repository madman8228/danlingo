## idioms: fetch a pretty penny
- example: That vintage Rolex watch my grandfather left me will fetch a pretty penny at the auction next month. || 我祖父留给我的那块 vintage 劳力士手表下个月在拍卖会上会卖个好价钱。
- example: The hand-carved mahogany furniture made by local artisans fetches a pretty penny in overseas markets. || 当地工匠制作的手工雕刻红木家具在海外市场售价很高。

## idioms: give and take
- example: A healthy marriage requires plenty of give and take from both partners, not just one person making all the compromises. || 一段健康的婚姻需要双方互相迁就，而不是只有一个人做出所有妥协。
- example: The success of the team project came down to give and take: each member adjusted their schedule to meet the collective deadline. || 团队项目的成功归根结底是互相忍让的结果：每个成员都调整了自己的日程来赶上集体截止日期。

## idioms: give someone a run for their money
- example: The young chess prodigy gave the reigning world champion a run for his money, pushing the match to a seventh and final round. || 这位年轻的国际象棋神童给现任世界冠军带来了不小的压力，把比赛拖到了第七局也是最后一局。
- example: The small local coffee shop gives the big chain stores a run for their money with its unique homemade pastries and personalized service. || 这家当地的小咖啡店凭借独特的自制糕点和个性化服务，完全能和大型连锁咖啡店一较高下。

## idioms: give someone the green light
- example: The city council finally gave the green light to the construction of the new community park, which will start next spring. || 市议会最终批准了新社区公园的建设，工程将于明年春天开工。
- example: My manager gave me the green light to launch the new marketing campaign we've been planning for months. || 我的经理同意我启动我们策划了好几个月的新营销活动。

## idioms: grab the bull by the horns
- example: Instead of waiting for the layoff announcement, John grabbed the bull by the horns and started sending out his resume to other companies. || 约翰没有坐等裁员通知，而是果断采取行动，开始向其他公司投递简历。
- example: When the team fell behind by two goals in the first half, the captain grabbed the bull by the horns and rallied the players to turn the game around. || 球队在上半场落后两球时，队长果断挺身而出，鼓舞队员们扭转了比赛局势。

## idioms: return like for like
- example: When he played a prank on me, I decided to return like for like and hid his favorite snack as a joke. || 他捉弄了我之后，我决定以牙还牙，把他最爱的零食藏起来当作玩笑。
- example: In international trade, some countries return like for like when unfair tariffs are imposed on their exports. || 在国际贸易中，当本国出口商品被征收不公平关税时，有些国家会采取对等报复措施。

## idioms: return the compliment
- example: She brought me a homemade cookie when she moved in, so I returned the compliment by helping her carry the heavy furniture up the stairs. || 她搬进来的时候给我带了一块自制曲奇，我帮她把沉重的家具搬上楼作为回礼。
- example: When the audience praised his performance, the actor returned the compliment by thanking them for their unwavering support over the years. || 观众称赞他的表演时，这位演员反过来感谢了观众多年来坚定不移的支持。

## idioms: return to the fold
- example: After working as a freelance designer for five years, she decided to return to the fold and accept a full-time position at her former company. || 做了五年自由设计师之后，她决定重回老东家，接受了一份全职岗位。
- example: The prodigal son returned to the fold on his 30th birthday, apologizing to his parents for all the trouble he had caused in his youth. || 这个浪子在他30岁生日那天浪子回头，为自己年轻时惹下的所有麻烦向父母道歉。

## idioms: seize the bull by the horns
- example: Instead of avoiding the conflict with his teammate, he decided to seize the bull by the horns and have an honest conversation to resolve the issue. || 他没有逃避和队友的矛盾，而是决定直面问题，和对方坦诚沟通来解决争端。
- example: When the startup faced a serious funding crisis, the founder seized the bull by the horns, cut unnecessary expenses, and pitched to dozens of investors within a week. || 当这家初创公司遭遇严重的融资危机时，创始人果断迎难而上，砍掉不必要的开支，一周之内就向几十位投资人做了项目推介。

## idioms: take someone's breath away
- example: The view of the snow-capped mountains reflected in the clear blue lake at sunrise took my breath away. || 日出时分，皑皑雪山倒映在澄澈的蓝湖中的景象美得令我窒息。
- example: When she walked down the aisle in her wedding dress, her beauty took everyone's breath away, including her groom who had tears in his eyes. || 她穿着婚纱走过通道时，她的美惊艳了所有人，包括眼含热泪的新郎。

## idioms: take the bull by the horns
- example: Instead of waiting for the manager to blame him for the project failure, Jack decided to take the bull by the horns and explain the whole situation honestly. || 杰克没有等经理为项目失败怪罪他，而是决定直面问题，诚实地解释了整个情况。
- example: When the virus outbreak first hit the small town, the local government took the bull by the horns and imposed strict quarantine measures to protect residents. || 当小镇首次暴发疫情时，当地政府果断应对，实施了严格的隔离措施来保护居民。

## idioms: take the cake
- example: I've seen many careless mistakes at work, but forgetting to send the core contract to the client really takes the cake. || 我在工作中见过很多粗心的错误，但忘记把核心合同发给客户也实在是太离谱了。
- example: Of all the silly birthday gifts my friend has ever given me, this custom mug with my awkward high school photo on it takes the cake for being the funniest. || 在我朋友送过我的所有奇葩生日礼物里，这个印着我高中傻照片的定制马克杯是最搞笑的。
