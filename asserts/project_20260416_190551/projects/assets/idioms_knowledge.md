## idioms: accept the inevitable
- example: When the floodwaters rose higher than the levees, the residents had to accept the inevitable and evacuate their homes. || 当洪水水位涨到高于堤坝时，居民们不得不接受无法避免的事实，撤离自己的家。
- example: After three failed attempts to fix the old computer, I finally accepted the inevitable and bought a new one. || 尝试修理这台旧电脑三次都失败后，我终于接受了不可避免的结果，买了台新的。

## idioms: agree to differ
- example: My sister likes pineapple on pizza but I hate it; we’ve decided to agree to differ instead of arguing about it every time we order takeout. || 我姐姐喜欢披萨上放菠萝，但我很讨厌，我们决定保留各自意见，而不是每次点外卖的时候都为此争吵。
- example: The two researchers agreed to differ on the interpretation of the data, as neither could present enough evidence to convince the other. || 两位研究员在数据的解读上同意保留各自意见，因为双方都拿不出足够的证据说服对方。

## idioms: receive into the fold
- example: After the new member completed the three-month probation period, the club officially received her into the fold. || 新成员完成三个月的试用期后，俱乐部正式接纳她成为团体的一员。
- example: The small town’s local church holds a special ceremony every spring to receive newly converted believers into the fold. || 这个小镇的当地教堂每年春天都会举办特别仪式，接纳新皈依的信徒成为教会的一员。

## idioms: refuse to take no for an answer
- example: The talented salesperson would refuse to take no for an answer, and kept adjusting his proposal until the client finally agreed to sign the contract. || 这位能力出众的销售人员不肯接受拒绝，不断调整自己的方案，直到客户最终同意签署合同。
- example: When my grandma insisted that I stay for dinner, she refused to take no for an answer, even though I told her I had to finish my work report that night. || 我奶奶坚持要我留下来吃晚饭，我说我当晚得赶工作报告也没用，她根本不肯听我的推辞。

## idioms: reject the null hypothesis
- example: After analyzing the survey data with statistical software, the research team decided to reject the null hypothesis that there was no correlation between sleep quality and work efficiency. || 用统计软件分析完调查数据后，研究团队决定拒绝“睡眠质量和工作效率不存在关联”的零假设。
- example: If the p-value of your experiment is lower than the preset significance level, you can reject the null hypothesis and conclude that your independent variable has a noticeable effect. || 如果你的实验p值低于预设的显著性水平，你就可以拒绝零假设，得出你的自变量存在显著影响的结论。
