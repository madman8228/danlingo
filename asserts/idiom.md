

## idiom: talk big
- examples:
  1. He always talks big about his connections with famous celebrities, but no one has ever seen him prove it. || 他总是吹嘘自己和名人有交情，但从来没人见过他拿出证据。
  2. Don't just talk big—show us what you can actually do to solve this problem. || 别光说大话，给我们看看你到底能做什么来解决这个问题。
  3. My little cousin loves to talk big about how he'll be a professional soccer player someday, even though he barely practices. || 我的小表弟总爱夸口说自己某天会成为职业足球运动员，尽管他几乎不训练。

## idiom: talk large
- examples:
  1. He always talks large about his supposed business connections, but no one has ever seen him actually close a deal. || 他总是大吹大擂自己所谓的商业人脉，但从来没人见过他真正做成过一笔生意。
  2. Don't be fooled by how she talks large during meetings—she rarely follows through on any of her grand promises. || 别被她在会议上的夸夸其谈骗了——她那些宏大的承诺几乎从来不会兑现。
  3. When we were teenagers, my cousin would talk large about all the adventures he planned to have, but he never even left our small town. || 我们十几岁的时候，我表哥总在大谈特谈他计划要去经历的各种冒险，但他连我们的小镇都从没离开过。

## idiom: make a huge splash
- examples:
  1. The new tech startup made a huge splash at the industry conference with its revolutionary AI product. || 这家新科技初创公司凭借其革命性的人工智能产品在行业大会上引起了巨大轰动。
  2. When the A-list actress announced her sudden retirement, it made a huge splash across social media. || 当这位一线女星宣布突然退休时，在社交媒体上掀起了轩然大波。
  3. The fast-food chain's limited-edition spicy burger made a huge splash, selling out within three hours of its launch. || 这家快餐连锁店的限量版辣味汉堡大获成功，推出仅三小时就销售一空。

## idiom: great things come in small packages
- examples:
  1. Don't underestimate that tiny portable speaker—it can fill the whole room with rich sound. Great things come in small packages! || 别小瞧那个迷你便携音箱，它能让整个房间都充满饱满的音效。真是浓缩的都是精华！
  2. My grandma's homemade strawberry jam is stored in a tiny glass jar, but it's bursting with fresh, sweet flavor. Truly, great things come in small packages. || 我奶奶自制的草莓果酱装在一个小小的玻璃罐里，但它充满了新鲜香甜的味道。真的是浓缩的都是精华。
  3. That compact smartphone has a powerful processor and an incredible camera—once again, great things come in small packages. || 那款小巧的智能手机有着强劲的处理器和出色的摄像头——这再次印证了浓缩的都是精华。

## idiom: a tiny drop in the ocean
- examples:
  1. Donating $100 to the global hunger relief fund is just a tiny drop in the ocean compared to the billions needed. || 相比缓解全球饥饿所需的数十亿美元，捐赠100美元只是沧海一粟。
  2. The amount of plastic we recycled last month is a tiny drop in the ocean when you consider the total plastic waste produced worldwide each day. || 对比全球每天产生的塑料垃圾总量，我们上个月回收的塑料量不过是九牛一毛。
  3. Even if every student in our school volunteers for an hour, it's a tiny drop in the ocean of work needed to clean up the entire national park. || 就算我们学校每个学生都志愿工作一小时，对于清理整个国家公园所需的工作量来说，也只是杯水车薪。

## idiom: Happy wife, happy life.
- examples:
  1. When my wife suggested we take a weekend trip to the coast, I didn't hesitate to agree—after all, happy wife, happy life. || 当我妻子提议我们去海边度周末时，我毫不犹豫地答应了——毕竟，老婆开心，生活顺心。
  2. My dad always says he’ll do whatever my mom wants because he firmly believes "happy wife, happy life." || 我爸爸总说我妈妈想做什么他都愿意，因为他坚信“老婆开心，生活顺心”。
  3. I decided to buy my wife that expensive skincare set she’s been eyeing; happy wife, happy life, right? || 我决定给我老婆买她觊觎已久的那款昂贵护肤套装，老婆开心，生活顺心，对吧？

## idiom: jump for joy
- examples:
  1. When I heard I got the scholarship, I jumped for joy and called my parents right away. || 当我听说自己拿到奖学金时，我高兴得跳了起来，立刻给父母打了电话。
  2. The kids jumped for joy when they saw the pile of presents under the Christmas tree. || 孩子们看到圣诞树下那堆礼物时，开心得蹦了起来。
  3. My grandma jumped for joy when she learned that her favorite grandson was coming to visit. || 奶奶得知她最疼爱的孙子要来看望她时，高兴得跳了起来。

## idiom: jump for delight
- examples:
  1. When Lila heard she got accepted into her dream university, she jumped for delight and hugged her parents tightly. || 当莉拉听说自己被梦想中的大学录取时，她高兴得跳了起来，紧紧抱住了父母。
  2. The little girl jumped for delight when she saw the fluffy puppy her parents had brought home as a birthday gift. || 当小女孩看到父母带回家作为生日礼物的那只毛茸茸的小狗时，她开心得蹦了起来。
  3. The whole team jumped for delight as soon as the final whistle blew, knowing they had won the championship. || 终场哨声一响，整个球队都高兴得跳了起来，因为他们知道自己赢得了冠军。

## idiom: sad dog
- examples:
  1. Don't be such a sad dog—cheer up, we've got tickets to your favorite band's concert! || 别这么垂头丧气的啦，振作起来，我们已经拿到你最喜欢的乐队演唱会门票了！
  2. After losing the championship, the star player turned into a sad dog, barely leaving his apartment for a week. || 输掉冠军赛后，那位明星球员变得一蹶不振，整整一周几乎没出过公寓。
  3. My little brother was a sad dog when his pet hamster passed away, but we helped him make a memory book to feel better. || 我的小弟弟在他的宠物仓鼠去世后闷闷不乐，但我们帮他做了一本纪念册，让他好受了一些。

## idiom: make oneself miserable over sth
- examples:
  1. There's no need to make yourself miserable over a small mistake that everyone has forgotten about. || 没必要为一个大家都已经忘了的小错误让自己难受。
  2. She tends to make herself miserable over every little comment her boss makes, even when it's not critical. || 她总因为老板说的每一句无关紧要的话就自我折磨，哪怕那些话根本不是批评。
  3. Don't make yourself miserable over the past; focus on the good things ahead instead. || 别为过去的事让自己痛苦不堪，还是多关注未来的美好吧。

## idiom: happy as a clam
- examples:
  1. Ever since she moved to the quiet coastal town, she's been as happy as a clam waking up to the sound of waves every morning. || 自从搬到这个宁静的海滨小镇，她每天早上在海浪声中醒来，过得无比惬意。
  2. My grandpa sits in his armchair reading newspapers and sipping tea every afternoon, looking as happy as a clam. || 我爷爷每天下午坐在扶手椅上看报纸、喝茶，看起来怡然自得。
  3. The kids played in the shallow pool for hours, splashing and laughing, as happy as a clam. || 孩子们在浅水池里玩了好几个小时，又泼又笑，开心得不得了。

## idiom: look on the bright side
- examples:
  1. I know you're upset about missing the concert, but look on the bright side—we can get front-row tickets for the rescheduled show! || 我知道你因为错过演唱会很沮丧，但往好的方面想——我们可以买到加场演出的前排票啊！
  2. When my car broke down on the way to work, I tried to look on the bright side and used the extra time to listen to my favorite podcast. || 我的车在上班路上抛锚时，我努力往好的方面想，利用多出的时间听了我最喜欢的播客。
  3. Losing the competition was tough, but look on the bright side—you gained a lot of valuable experience and made new friends. || 输掉比赛确实不好受，但往好的方面想——你收获了很多宝贵的经验，还交到了新朋友。

## idiom: sadder but wiser
- examples:
  1. After getting scammed out of his savings, he was sadder but wiser, vowing never to trust get-rich-quick schemes again. || 被骗光积蓄后，他吃一堑长一智，发誓再也不相信一夜暴富的骗局了。
  2. The breakup left her heartbroken, but she was sadder but wiser when it came to choosing partners who respected her boundaries. || 分手让她心碎，但在选择尊重她底线的伴侣这件事上，她变得吃一堑长一智。
  3. Having failed the exam due to procrastination, the student was sadder but wiser and started a strict study schedule right away. || 因为拖延而考试不及格后，这个学生吃一堑长一智，立刻制定了严格的学习计划。
